Paragraphs

Nature, freedom, and transcendence are some of the most important themes of ‘Ode to a Nightingale,’ the first poem on our list.
It is likely one of Keats’ most well-known poems and in it, the nightingale plays an important role. The speaker listens to it sing
and feels jealous of its carefree life. Inspired by the song, he considers giving himself over to the woods and trying to seek out
the same kind of freedom the nightingale has. He even thinks about allowing himself to die in the woods. This beautiful vision
that the speaker is entranced by is broken when the bird flies off and leaves the speaker alone. The confusion he felt at the
beginning of the poem returns and he is unable to determine what was real and what was a dream.
